What Are
Machine Readable Brands?

Definition · July 2026 · Robert Haase

Definition

Machine Readable Brands are brands whose identity, language, and design logic exist as a specification that people and systems can work with equally. I coined the term in 2026, in Brand Infrastructure.

The background is simple: brands have acquired a second audience. AI search summarizes them, agents compare and recommend them, teams produce in their name with AI tools. All three work only with what they can process. A brand book describes a brand. A specification can be applied. That difference decides whether a brand exists in systems or only in presentations.

The Three Stages

A brand becomes machine readable in three stages. The third one makes the difference.

→ Stage one: being read. AI search and agents find the brand, summarize it correctly, and can recommend it. Whether a brand reaches this stage is measurable: Agentic Brand Readiness is the maturity level for it, self-test included.
→ Stage two: producing with AI. Teams generate on-brand output because the brand exists as a specification, not as an invitation to interpret. Without this stage, AI production sounds like no one or like everyone.
→ Stage three: becoming applicable. The brand becomes a logic that systems work with directly: language, claims, facts, approvals, boundaries. Brands at this stage are Machine Readable Brands.

What Can Be Structured, and What Cannot

Not everything about a brand belongs in a specification, and that is not the goal.

Meaning, attitude, and trust remain what they are: human, told, experienced. What can be structured are the parts that must be applied repeatedly and consistently: the positioning in one sentence, the language rules, the verifiable facts, the design system, the boundaries of what may be promised in the brand's name. Who defines what an agent may promise, and who stands behind it, is covered in Agent Authority.

Boundaries

Three common mix-ups.

SEO and GEO optimize content for findability. Machine Readable Brands start earlier: with the brand itself as an applicable structure. Findability is a consequence, not the goal. The brand book describes the brand for people, usually as a PDF; it can be read but not applied. The design system structures the execution layer — components, colors, typography — and is often already public and versioned. What is missing is the same structure for the layer of meaning: positioning, language, decision logic. That is exactly where Machine Readable Brands begin.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Machine Readable Brands?
Brands whose identity, language, and design logic exist as a specification that people and systems can work with equally. Instead of a brand book that describes, there is a structured brand definition that AI search, agents, and production tools can apply directly.

Who coined the term?
I coined the term in 2026, in the article Brand Infrastructure, as the third stage of a maturity model: being read, producing with AI, becoming applicable.

How is this different from SEO or GEO?
SEO and GEO optimize content for findability. Machine Readable Brands define the brand itself as an applicable structure. The technology is the entry ticket; the brand definition is the substance.

How does a brand start?
First test the current state, for example with the Agentic Brand Readiness self-test. Then formulate the brand as a specification: who it is, what it stands for, how it speaks, which facts hold. And make that definition machine-readable and maintained, via structured data, llms.txt, and versioned documents.
